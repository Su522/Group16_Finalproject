Overview

In this presentation, we explain how AI can help prevent disease from spreading in human society, mainly using AI for tracking human activities and generating predictions of disease spread.

Usage of AI

BlueDot, the first AI to detect the COVID-19 outbreak, identified unusual pneumonia cases in Wuhan on December 31, 2019, by analyzing local news, health data, and flight patterns. It predicted the virus's global spread through airline traffic analysis, alerting clients and officials days before announcements.

Problem

  • AI analysis may require monitoring specific groups, which could infringe on privacy.
  • The public may doubt the accuracy and transparency of AI systems, reducing trust.

Solution

  • Use Bluetooth to estimate relative distances instead of GPS tracking.
  • Enhance transparency by sharing AI decision-making processes openly.

Future

More accurate models for predicting outbreaks, early detection of unknown viruses, and personalized treatments and prevention plans.
